# **So What's Computational Thinking?**
***

In my line of thinking, it's to prevent this.

![as always: relevant xkcd](https://imgs.xkcd.com/comics/tasks.png)

Wikipedia defines it as (taken from Jeannette M. Wing): 

*Computational Thinking is a set of problem-solving methods that involve expressing problems and their solutions in ways that a computer could execute.*

UD created a general rubric that splits CT into four main pillars:

* Decomposition
    * Breaks a problem into its constituent subproblems
* Data
    * Analyze (or create) a data set that facilitates discovery of patterns and relationships
* Algorithms
    * Creates a series of ordered steps to solve a problem or achive a goal
* Abstraction
    * Reduces complexity to create a general representation of a process or group of objects so it is not only appropriate for the immediate purpose or goal but can also be used in different contexts

When I attended Computational Thinking conference after I was accepted to the program, one gentlemen raised his hand and asked "How is Computational Thinking different from normal problem solving skills?" It was a valid question, since on the surface both look like a solid framework for approaching a problem.
